Getting started

Email accounts

Add one or more email accounts. Each account has its own color and notification settings.

Adding a new account

1
Open Settings → Accounts

Tap the person icon in the bottom navigation.

2
Tap "+ Add account"

Button in the bottom-right corner of the Accounts screen.

3
Choose login type

Gmail and Microsoft 365 offer OAuth2. For other providers, enter IMAP/SMTP details manually.

4
Choose color and display name

Each account gets a unique color shown next to messages for quick identification.

Manual IMAP/SMTP setup

You'll need these details from your email provider:

IMAP servere.g. imap.gmail.com
IMAP port993 (SSL) or 143 (STARTTLS)
SMTP servere.g. smtp.gmail.com
SMTP port465 (SSL) or 587 (STARTTLS)
UsernameUsually the full email address
💡
App password

Some providers (e.g. Gmail, Yahoo, Seznam) require an "app password" when you have two-factor authentication enabled. Generate it in your provider's security settings — do not use your regular login password.

Managing active accounts

In Settings → Accounts you can edit, deactivate or delete accounts. The "Select active accounts" button lets you choose which accounts sync.

⚠️
Free version: 2 active accounts limit

After the trial expires or without Premium, you can have at most 2 active accounts. Others remain saved but won't sync. Premium removes this limit entirely.

Account colors

Each account has a color visible next to messages and in notifications. Change it anytime in account settings.

Getting started

Login & OAuth

Secure login for Gmail, Microsoft 365 and other providers.

Gmail — OAuth2 login

TchikiMail opens Google's official login page in a built-in browser. Your password never reaches the app.

1
Add account → Gmail

Tap "Sign in with Google".

2
Sign in on Google's page

Enter your Google credentials there.

3
Grant permissions

Confirm the requested permissions and you'll be redirected back to the app.

Microsoft 365 / Outlook

Same principle as Google — through Microsoft's login page. Works for Outlook.com, Hotmail, Live and corporate Microsoft 365 accounts.

ℹ️
Automatic token refresh

OAuth2 access tokens expire periodically. TchikiMail refreshes them automatically in the background — you won't need to re-enter your password.

IMAP/SMTP — password login

For other providers you enter your password directly. It is encrypted with AES-256-GCM and stored in the Android Keystore.

⚠️
Transferring to a new device

Encryption keys are device-specific. When restoring on a new phone, you'll need to re-enter IMAP/SMTP passwords. Gmail and Microsoft 365 just need to log in again via OAuth2.

Email

Search

Instant full-text search across all stored emails.

What is searched

Tap the magnifying glass to open search. Covers subject, sender name, email address and message body.

How search works

A local full-text index of downloaded messages is maintained. Search is instant and works offline.

💡
Tip

Older messages must be loaded first (pull-to-refresh or "Load older"). Only then are they indexed and visible in search results.

Settings

Synchronization

How and when TchikiMail downloads new emails.

Automatic sync

Background sync via Android WorkManager. Minimum interval: 15 minutes (Android system limit). Sync resumes automatically after device restart.

Manual sync

Pull down the message list (pull-to-refresh) for an immediate sync.

Outbox queue

Messages pending send are queued and sent automatically. If sending fails (no internet), the message stays in the queue and retries later.

💡
Per-account notifications

Enable or disable new-message notifications for each account separately in Settings → Accounts → [account].

Settings

Security

How TchikiMail protects your data and credentials.

Password encryption

IMAP/SMTP passwords are encrypted with AES-256-GCM and stored in the Android Keystore, protected by your device's hardware.

App lock

TchikiMail supports locking the app with a fingerprint or screen lock gesture. Configure it in Settings → Security → App lock.

What is stored locally

✅ Encrypted in KeystoreIMAP/SMTP passwords, OAuth tokens
✅ Local databaseDownloaded emails, attachments, contacts
⚠️ Not auto-backed upPasswords & tokens (device-specific keys)
ℹ️
Anonymous trial data

To protect the trial period, an anonymous device hash is stored on Firebase servers in Europe. It contains no personal data — only the first installation timestamp.

Lost or reset device

Your emails are safe on the server. Reinstall TchikiMail, sign in to your accounts, and restore settings from an exported backup. IMAP/SMTP passwords must be re-entered.
